\item Add the numerators, writing the answer over a single denominator.

Multiply. 


$$
\left( -\frac{7}{3}\right)\left( -6\right)
$$
Write your answer in simplest form.




%type problem above this line
\ifbool{answerKey}%
{\textbf{Explanation:}
\begin {itemize}
\item A whole number can be written as a fraction with a denominator of $1$, so rewrite $−6$.
$$-\frac{7}{3} \times \left( \frac{-6}{1}\right)$$
\item Negative × negative = positive, so change the signs.
$$\frac{7}{3} \times \frac{6}{1}$$
\item Multiply numerator to numerator, and denominator to denominator.
$$\frac{42}{3}$$
\item Simplify by dividing with common factors.
$$\frac{42 \div 3}{3 \div 3}$$
\end {itemize}}
\vfill \textbf{Answer:}

$$ \frac {-1 \times -4 \times 3}{-9 \times 8 \times 1}$$
$$ \frac{12}{-72}$$
\item Simplify by dividing with common factors.
$$ \frac{12 \div 12}{-72 \div 12}$$
\end {itemize}}
